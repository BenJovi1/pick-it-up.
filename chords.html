Here are some of the most essential chords for beginners to learn on the guitar. Practice these chords and try to switch between them smoothly.

Essential chords

  • G major
  • C major
  • D major
  • E minor
  • A minor
  • F major

Most songs can be played using just these chords, so mastering them will give you a solid foundation for playing the guitar.

Chord diagrams

Chord diagrams are visual representations of how to play chords on the guitar. They show you where to place your fingers on the fretboard.

The numbered circles indicate which fingers to use so when says one you would use your index finger, when it says 2 you would use your middle finger,
When it says 3 you would use your ring finger and if it says 4 you would use your pinkie finger.
The x above the string means you don't play that string and the o above the string means you play that string open.
If there is a line it means that you lay down a finger across all strings, playing a barre chord (wich are very advanced).

Strumming patterns

Strumming patterns are the rhythms you use to play chords.
Often times this will be symbolized by up and down arrows.
When the arrow points up you will strum from the high e string to the low e string.
When the arrow points down you will strum from the low e string the the high e string. Songs typically end in a downstrum.

Practice switching between these chords to build muscle memory and improve your playing skills.
